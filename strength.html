What is Strength Training?

Strength training involves exercises designed to improve your strength and endurance by working against resistance. It is a vital component of overall fitness, helping to build muscle mass, boost metabolism, and enhance overall physical performance. Strength training can also improve bone density, reduce the risk of injury, and promote better posture. Whether you are lifting weights, using resistance bands, or doing bodyweight exercises, strength training plays an essential role in achieving a healthy and active lifestyle.

Push-ups

Push Ups are a classic bodyweight exercise that strengthens the chest, shoulders, triceps and core muscles. This exercise is great for building upper body strength and endurance while working your core muscles.

Squats

Squats focus on building leg strength while working the core and back muscles. Squats are a basic lower body exercise that targets the quads, hamstrings, and calves. It also improves balance, and stability.

Plank

Plank is an isometric exercise that challenges the entire core of your body, including the abdominal muscles, lumbar muscles, and lower back. Holding the plank position requires full-body tension and focus

Weightlifting

Weightlifting is strength training that involves lifting weights to build muscle and increase physical strength. This form of exercise not only improves muscle tone, but also increases metabolism, bone density, and overall endurance.

Pull Up

Pull-ups are an excellent upper body exercise that targets the back, shoulders and arms. This compound movement requires you to pull your body up onto the bar until your chin is above the bar. It is an effective way to improve grip strength and build upper body muscle mass.

Lunges

Lunges are a powerful lower body exercise that helps strengthen the quads, hamstrings, glutes and calves. To perform a lunge, step forward with one foot and lower your body until both knees are bent at about 90 degrees. Return to standing and repeat with the other leg.
